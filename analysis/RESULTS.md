## Honesty statement

We do not have real student study-history + full-length-score longitudinal data, so we grade the *steps of the bridge*: (1) memory calibration, (2) held-out performance prediction, (3) a documented score mapping with a range. The readiness coefficients are fit on a synthetic generative model and are **not yet field-calibrated**. Per Speedrun sec. 9 this honest position is preferred over a fabricated score.

### 2b. Memory->Performance bridge on real matched items (the differentiated core)

> Real recall+transfer MC items (`data/questions/bridge_pilot.json`), real graded responses. Respondents: ['llm:llama3'] (60 paired obs, 12 concepts). No paywalled data, no fabrication.

- Recall **0.833** vs transfer **0.583** -> **gap 0.25**.
- **P(transfer correct | recall correct) = 0.5** - recalling the fact does NOT guarantee applying it.
- McNemar exact test: 25 recalled-but-not-applied vs 10 the reverse, **p = 0.0167**.
- corr(recall_rate, transfer_rate) across concepts = **-0.378** -> memory and performance are separable abilities.
- **Bridge supported: True.** Human respondents (add via `perf_bridge.py quiz <name>`) extend the claim.

## Limitations

- Synthetic data; replace with real review logs + practice-test scores for field calibration.
- Readiness coefficients not yet validated against real MCAT outcomes.
- Ablation is a literature-anchored simulation, not a live learner study.
- Graph recommender is evaluated on a pre-registered prerequisite-learning model, not live learners; it demonstrates the recommender exploits prerequisite structure the baselines cannot.
- Real-item paraphrase outcomes are model-projected (persona ability x item difficulty); the prompt text and difficulty ratings are real/hand-authored.
